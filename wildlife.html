Wildlife of India

Explore the Rich Diversity of India's Flora and Fauna

The Majestic Bengal Tiger

The Bengal tiger is one of the most iconic and endangered species of big cats in India. Known for their powerful build and distinctive orange and black stripes, Bengal tigers are found in various national parks and reserves across India, such as the Sundarbans and Ranthambhore.

The Incredible Indian Elephant

The Indian elephant is a key symbol of Indian culture and wildlife. These gentle giants are found in forests across the country, from the Western Ghats to Assam. Unfortunately, their numbers are dwindling due to habitat loss and poaching.

The Majestic Peacock

India's national bird, the peacock, is a symbol of beauty and grace. With its vibrant feathers and elaborate courtship dance, the peacock is an iconic representation of India's wildlife and culture. It can be spotted in forests, grasslands, and even in urban areas.

The Mighty Indian Rhinoceros

The Indian rhinoceros, also known as the one-horned rhinoceros (Rhinoceros unicornis), is a large herbivorous mammal native to the Indian subcontinent. It is recognized by its thick, armor-like skin folds and a single horn on its snout.
